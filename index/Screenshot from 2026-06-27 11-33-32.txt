Artificial Intelligence (Al) is a branch of computer science focused on building
machines and software capable of performing tasks that typically require human
intelligence. These tasks include learning from data, recognizing patterns,
understanding language, solving problems, and making decisions. 18\+3

Unlike traditional computer programs that follow rigid, step-by-step instructions
programmed by humans, Al systems analyze massive amounts of information to
identify trends and adapt over time. @ Michigan Technological University +1

How Al Works

Al relies on a few foundational technologies to simulate human reasoning:

* Machine Learning (ML): A foundational subset of Al where computers are trained
on large datasets rather than explicitly programmed. By seeing millions of
examples, the Al learns to perform a specific task (like recognizing a face or
translating a language). @ Southern New Hampshire University +2

* Deep Learning: A more complex form of ML that uses neural networks to mimic th
human brain, allowing Al to process highly unstructured data like audio or visual
information. &

+ Large Language Models (LLMs): The engines behind modern conversational Al.
