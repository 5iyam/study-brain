A route is a designated path, course, or way taken to get from one place to another.
It can refer to physical infrastructure (like a highway or transit line), a regular sequence
of stops for deliveries, or a method for achieving a goal. © Meriam-webster +3

Common Definitions

+ Physical Path: A specific way or direction between places (e.g., the quickest route
to the airport).

+ Transit Line: A fixed course traveled regularly by a bus, train, airline, or ship.

* Work Circuit: An established list of stops or clients visited regularly for a job (e.g. a
mail carrier's route).

* Method/Process: A chosen course of action used to achieve a specific result or
goal (e.g., a route to success).

+ Computing: In information technology, to direct or forward data (such as emails or
network traffic) to a specific destination. @ cambridge Dictionary +4
